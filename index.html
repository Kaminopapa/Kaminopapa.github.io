SABOR VENEZOLANO

  • AREPA

    Arepa is a type of food made of ground maize dough, eaten in the northern region of South America since pre-Columbian times, and notable primarily in the cuisine of Colombia and Venezuela, but also present in the cuisines of Bolivia and other countries.

  • CACHAPA

    Cachapa is a traditional dish made from maize flour from Venezuela. Like arepas, they are popular at roadside stands. They can be made like pancakes of fresh corn dough, or wrapped in dry corn leaves and boiled (cachapa de hoja). The most common varieties are made with fresh ground corn mixed into a thick batter and cooked on a budare, like pancakes; the cachapa is slightly thicker and lumpier because of the pieces from corn kernels.

  • EMPANADA

    Venezuelan Empanadas are fried corn flour patties stuffed with different fillings such as cheese, ham, beans, pulled steak, chicken or fish.

Avila Caracas
If you are visiting Avila for the first time, you can take a ride up the mountain to preserve your strength and enjoy the scenery. The cable car route is very long. It takes two peaks to cross, Caracas is gradually smaller in sight.
Angel Falls
A slice of heaven on earth? Canaima Park is it, untouched beautiful nature as far as the eye can see!!! Angel Falls is of course AMAZING, but in my opinion the true beauty is Canaima Lagoon. WoW WoW WoW.
Cayo De Agua
The clearest water I've ever seen!! Recommende if you go on a day tour, provided that the tour organizer will take care to include everything (lunch, snack, seats, water & other drinks).
Monte Roraima
Wonderful hike.Roraima trek was the best I have experienced so far. Several days outside the civilization on fresh air was exactly what I had hoped for also Gran Sabana landscape and nature is piece of a kind.
Parque Nacional Morrocoy
People here are paying attention to the environment, beaches are clean and you can still have silence if you look for a lonely cayo
Médanos de Coro
Dunes + Wind = Amazing Experience. I felt as if I was in the Sahara Desert. Be prepared for loads of sand in your shoes..... You can access the dunes taking the road to Punto Fijo or ask where the "Estatua de la Madre" is.
