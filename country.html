Regional location

Venezuela is bordered by the Caribbean
Sea and the Atlantic Ocean to the north,
Guyana to the east, Brazil to the south,
Colombia to the west, and Aruba, the
Netherlands Antilles (both the Dutch
autonomous territories) to the outside of
Venezuela. Island states such as
Trinidad and Tobago.

Some thing you dont know

Venezuela’s country name “Venezuela” is
actually derived from the Italian word
“Little Venice”. Its allusion was discovered by the
Italian explorer Amerigo Vespucci when he
saw the American Indo at Lake Maracaibo. The waters
of the high-rise houses in which the Anren
lived are named after the waters of Europe.

Climatic characteristics

In addition to the mountains, the
territory is basically a savanna climate.
The temperature varies with precipitation
and ground level. The mountains are
mild and the lowlands are hot. The annual
average temperature is 26 to 28 degrees
Celsius. The annual average precipitation
increases from 500 mm to 3000 mm from the
northern coast to the south. In the upper
reaches of the Orinoco River with the most
precipitation, the annual average precipi-
tation is over 3,000 mm. In the driest area on
the northern coast, the average annual pre-
cipitation in the cities of La Guaira and Maracay
is only 550 mm. The whole season is rainy
season from June to November, and the dry season is
from December to May.
